Project Aurora — telehealth with heart

Role: UX Researcher & Product Designer Timeline: 10 weeks Tools: FigJam, Figma, Maze, Notion

Co-created a compassionate telehealth experience that supports new parents with personalised care pathways, proactive check-ins, and a library of warm, trustworthy resources.

Challenge

New parents often feel overwhelmed when navigating remote care. Clinics provide a patchwork of portals, forms and chatbots that rarely connect. Our challenge was to design an onboarding-to-support journey that feels cohesive, personal and calming.

Approach

  • Mapped end-to-end service blueprint after interviewing six first-time parents and two midwives.
  • Co-facilitated journey mapping workshop with client stakeholders to align on moments that matter most.
  • Prototyped adaptive onboarding that adjusts care modules based on family goals, wellbeing check-ins and support preferences.
  • Ran moderated usability tests, iterating microcopy and dashboard layout to reduce overwhelm.

Outcome

The final prototype guided parents through compassionate onboarding, included a calming “today” overview with three focus tasks, and surfaced human support through easy scheduling and chat. Usability testing showed a 42% increase in task confidence and interviews highlighted how the tone “felt like a warm hug.”
